Definition of Child Abuse

Child Abuse: The physical or verbal maltreatment or sexual molestation of a child

Long Term Effects of Child Abuse

Some of the long termeffects of child abuse has on children can drastically effect a child physically, mentally and socially that can continue on with them through adulthood. Some of the health problems that adults who have experienced child abuse are more prone to:

Physical Health Problems

  • Diabetes
  • Arthritis
  • Stroke
  • Heart disease

    • Mental Health Problems

  • Personality disorders
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
  • Depression
  • Anxiety disorders

    • Ways We Can Prevent Child Abuse

    We can prevent child abuse in many ways, some of which include:

  • Showing the public how serious child abuse truly is
  • Informing the public the different types of child abuse
    •
      By showing the public how serious child abuse really is and how drastic events that happened in a child's early life could effect a person, hopefully the eyes of the public will open up and realize how serious this situation truly is. By informing the public about the different types of child abuse that many don't know about or consider one. An example of a neglected form of child abuse is verbal/emotional child abuse. If the public knows about this form of child abuse maybe there will be more instances where a child is saved from this form of child abuse before it affects them.
